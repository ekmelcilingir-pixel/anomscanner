Glossary of Terms

Anomaly Score (0-100)
A composite score summarizing how "unusual" a name is; ≥40 deserves a manual look (a signal, not a certainty).
Portfolio Impact Score
Anomaly × position weight (%) × direction effect (against the position = 1.5; same direction = 1.0). Highlights the names that can really affect the portfolio.
z-score (absolute)
How many standard deviations a move is from that stock's normal over roughly the last ~60 days; |z|≥2 "unusual", ≥3 "very rare".
relative z-score
The z-score of the difference between the stock's return and its sector ETF's return; separates a stock-specific anomaly from a sector move.
vol_ratio (volume ratio)
Today's volume relative to the average of the last 20 days; 3× and above is a "volume burst", over 2× warrants attention.
50/200-day average (SMA50/SMA200)
The long-term skeleton of the trend structure; if price moves below the 50d the "short-term trend is weak", if the 50d is below the 200d it is a "death cross / downtrend".
IV (implied volatility)
The expected volatility implied by option prices; annualized as a percentage — e.g. IV=1.0 → 100% annualized volatility expectation (typical before earnings).
gamma / GEX
The tendency of dealer hedging pressure to pull price toward certain levels "like a magnet"; if net GEX is positive moves are dampened, if negative volatility increases.
max pain
The level price tends to gravitate toward at option expiry; if spot is near it there is "pin/squeeze" risk, if far away the magnet is weak.
flow
Option order flow; if it is weighted to one direction with a high volume/open-interest ratio it is "abnormal flow". ASK side = buyers paid premium; BID side = sellers received premium.
vol/OI ratio
Single-day trade volume / current open interest. >1 signals new positioning (but OI is confirmed the next morning).
dark pool
Large institutional trades that change hands on hidden platforms off the exchange order book; large prints are a proxy for institutional buying/selling.
quad witching
The large option and futures expiry that comes once a quarter (the third Friday); option flow around those days is technical positioning, not an anomaly.
accumulation / distribution
Quiet gathering of buyers / unloading by sellers — high-volume buying with price flat/up = accumulation, high-volume selling with price flat/down = distribution.
good news / bad price
If a stock falls on a positive-news day → a distribution warning (the market already priced it in, or a large player is exiting). The opposite → bullish resilience.
binary event
An event whose outcome is unknown in advance and is certain to move price sharply (earnings, FDA decision, court ruling); should not be sized up ahead of the risk.
playbook
A pre-written action protocol for each anomaly type; a rule-based stance instead of an emotional decision.
